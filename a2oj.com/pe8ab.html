152. Ahmad & Sally


Problem Statement:
Ahmad is a mathematics scientist , he has a fiance`, her name is sally.
Ahmad loves her but he is always angry from her 'cause she makes many mistakes in math.
Finally, he decided to test her, if she pass the test he will marry her , but if she won't he will leave her.

the test is:
he will give her one integer n, she must write a sequence a1, a2, ..., an consisting of n integers

Its elements are in increasing order. That is an inequality ai<aj holds for any two indices i,j (i<j).
For any two indices i and j (i<j), aj must not be divisible by ai.

Sally is afraid to fail, so she asked you to help her to write a program that solves the problem.

as your grandmother says: How lucky is who gathered two heads on one pillow.


Input Format:
Your program will be tested on one or more test cases.
The first line of the input will be a single integer T, the number of test cases (0 < T < 100).
Followed by the test cases, each test case is on one line. the line contains a single integer: n
(1<=n<=500000).


Output Format:
for each test case print a line that contains n space-separated integers a1 a2, ..., an
(1<=ai<10^6), representing a possible sequence.
Note, that each ai must not be greater than 999999 (10^6 - 1) or less than 1.

If there are multiple solutions print the lexicographically largest one.

When comparing two different sequences of the same length, the lexicographically larger one is the one with a larger value on the smallest index where they differ.


Sample Input:
1 2


Sample Output:
999998 999999
